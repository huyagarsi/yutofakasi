He Rose to Fame With His Appearance in the Sitcom, George Lopez

Luis Armand Garcia got his big break in 2002 at the age of 10 when he got the opportunity to feature in the highly acclaimed TV series George Lopez wherein he starred as Max. George Lopez is an American TV series that aired on Prime Time Television from 27th March 2002 to 8th May 2007. It is currently in syndication on the American pay TV network, TBS.

Produced by Fortis Films and Mohawk Productions in association with Warner Bros. Television, the series aired for six seasons and consisted of 120 episodes. Its executive producers are George Lopez, Deborah Oppenheimer, Bruce Helford, and Sandra Bullock.

In the series, comedian George Lopez plays the title character whose life revolves around his work and raising his family. George Lopez is married to Angie Palmero and Max (Luis) is their second child and only son.

Max also shares George’s sense of humor and will usually make decisions for himself based on his father’s actions. The series became an expected success as many markets moved it from overnight timeslots to more desirable ones.

The George Lopez show gave Luis Armand Garcia wide recognition, boosted his career and established him as an actor even at a very young age. It also earned him a nomination at the Young Artist Award for Best Performance in a TV Series (Comedy or Drama) – Young Actor Age Ten or Younger.
