    Cameron Mathison Career

    After graduating from McGill, Mathison began working as a model and started acting in commercials in Canada, the U.S., and Europe. This led to taking acting classes and the beginning of his acting profession in Toronto. He made his feature film debut in 54.

    Mathison followed up this appearance with a leading role in the Canadian independent movie Washed Up. About this time, December 1997, he was hired to play the role of Ryan Lavery on All My Children filming his first scenes on December 15. He first appeared on-screen in the January 12, 1998 episode.

    In 1999, Mathison won the Soap Opera Digest Award for Outstanding Male Newcomer and earned Daytime Emmy Award nominations for Outstanding Supporting Actor in 2002 and 2005. He was named by People magazine as one of “America’s 100 Most Eligible Bachelors” in 2000.

    Mathison was on season five of Dancing with the Stars in 2007 where he placed 8th with pro-Edyta Śliwińska. After working freelance with Good Morning America for several years, he signed with GMA in January 2009 as a regular correspondent.

    Mathison worked on many events for GMA: The Grammys, Oscars, Golden Globes, American Music Awards, MTV Video Music Awards, MTV Movie Awards, and the Country Music Awards. He also hosted the GMA Live Halloween specials as well as other live events.

    Mathison continued to work as a host and correspondent for GMA, the Emmy-nominated series Ultimate Proposal and the renovation reality show Game of Homes.

    Mathison also continues to act in both comedic and dramatic roles. Mathison has appeared on Castle, Desperate Housewives, Hot in Cleveland, Drop Dead Diva, the EXes, and numerous independent and television movies.

    Mathison joined the Hallmark/Crown Media family in 2014. He signed on to appear in 9 movies based on the Murder She Baked novels starring Alison Sweeney.

    Mathison signed with CBS’s Entertainment Tonight in April 2015. He is currently serving as a full-time correspondent and weekend co-anchor for the entertainment news magazine.

    Mathison was named the new co-host of Home and Family on July 26, 2018, together with Debbie Matenopoulos; he assumed his position in September with the launch of the seventh season.
