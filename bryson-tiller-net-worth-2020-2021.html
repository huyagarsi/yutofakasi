Career

Bryson Tiller’s debut mixtape titled ‘Killer Instinct Vol.1.’ which came out in 2011, was met with considerable appreciation and encouraged the rapper to release his single ‘Don’t’ officially. In 2015, he re-released the old single (which he had previously shared on his SoundCloud page) on iTunes, a decision that turned out to be in his favor. The single went on to reach no.

Thirteen on the Billboard Hot 100 list, brought him to the attention of various acclaimed artists and catapulted him to worldwide fame and recognition. He was immediately signed by RCA Records and also found a place in the Rolling Stone’s 2015 list of “10 New Artists You Need to Know”.

The year 2015, however, was still far from over. His debut studio album ‘Trapsoul’ came out in the same year, reached no.8 on the Billboard 200, and became one of the greatest albums of 2015, thanks to hitting songs, like ‘Exchange’ and ‘Sorry Not Sorry’ among others.

The year 2017 saw the release of some of his most impressive singles, like ‘Honey,’ ‘Get Mine,’ and ‘Something Tells Me’ before the grand release of his 2nd studio album called ‘True to Self’ which went on to break all records. It became his first album to debut at no.1 on the Billboard 200!

Bryson Tiller also has some hit collaborations under his belt with famed artists, like DJ Khaled, Future, Travis Scott, and Rihanna. 2017 single ‘Wild Thoughts,’ which Bryson sang for DJ Khaled along with Rihanna, was a blockbuster hit both in America and the U.K.

Awards & Achievements

Bryson Tiller’s achievements were recognized by the mayor of his hometown (Louisville) Greg Fischer in a 2016 event where the singer was bestowed with a key to the city of Louisville. The mayor also announced March 12 to be celebrated as ‘Bryson Tiller’s Day,’ an honor witnessed by few artists at such a young age. In the same year, Bryson bagged the BET awards for both Best New Artist and Best Male R&B/Pop Artist.
