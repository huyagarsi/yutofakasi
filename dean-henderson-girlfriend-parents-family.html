Dean Henderson Wiki: Parents & Family Life

Born as Dean Bradley Henderson on 12 March 1997, he is 24 years of age. He was raised by his parents in Whitehaven, Cumbria.


Dean has an elder brother Calum Henderson and a younger brother named Kyle Henderson. One is six years older and the other is a year older than him.

The 6-foot-2-inch used to play football with his brothers in his home. Moreover, his parents used to throw balls for him on the cul-de-sac at his home to improve his crossing skill.

His Professional Journey To Manchester United

Dean first joined the Carlisle United academy in 2005 and spent six years there. He joined the Manchester United academy at the age of 14 in 11.

After playing with the youth team for years, he signed his first professional contract with the United in August 2015. Afterward, he moved to National League North club Stockport County on a loan.

Moreover, Henderson joined League Two club Grimsby Town on a loan until January 2017. Later, he moved to the League One team Shrewsbury Town on loan for the 2017–18 season.

Following his successful loan spell, he signed a two-year contract extension with his parent club in June 2018. Afterward, he was loaned to the Championship side Sheffield United for the 2018–19 season.


With the impressive season, he signed a new contract with Manchester United until June 2022. Furthermore, he returned on loan to Sheffield United until the end of the 2019–20 season.

Dean was included in Manchester United’s senior squad list for the first time on 14 August 2020. He then signed a 5-year contract with the team, worth £26,000,000.


Henderson made his Champions League debut for United against İstanbul Başakşehir on 4 November 2020. Similarly, he made his Premier League debut as a substitute for the injured David de Gea against Southampton on 29 November.
